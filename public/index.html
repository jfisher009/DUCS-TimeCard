DTT - DUCS Time Tracker Application

The DTT App provides the Drury Computer Science community with the ability to record and track the time students spend on various project activities. The app can be used by both students and instructors.

Students

You can use the DTT app to easily record the time you spend at one setting working on a project. No need to jot a note to use at the end of week when you record your times in a time sheet to submit. With the DTT app all you need when your are finished with a work session is to do open your phone, login and record the information for your session. It works the same way with your tablet or computer as it does with your phone.

To get started, your instructor will send you an email inviting you to join your project. Don't have a note, ask you instructor to signup to use the DTT App for your project!

Instructors

Allow your students to easily record the time they spend on various project activities by using the DTT app. Your students will be able use their phone, tablet, or laptop to record work sessions as they finish a session working on the project. Work session information includes the start time, the finish time, total time worked in a session, description of the activity, and a classification of the activity. You will be able to view weekly time cards for each student or a project summary for all team members.

To get started request an account by clicking login and using the create an instructor account link. More information on using the app with a single project or multiple projects can be found on the instructor page after logging in.

About

The DUCS Time Tracker app is a project developed by the Spring 2021 Application Development class and the class instructor Scott Sigman. The application was developed at Drury University.
